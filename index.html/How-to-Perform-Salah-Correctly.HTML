How to Perform Salah Correctly

Introduction

Salah (prayer) is one of the most important pillars of Islam. Performing it correctly is essential for spiritual growth and obedience to Allah. Below is a detailed guide for performing Salah correctly step by step.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Make Wudu (Ablution): Ensure your body, hands, face, and feet are clean. Wudu is required before performing Salah.
    Tip: Always perform Wudu with focus and sincerity.
  2. Face the Qibla: Stand facing the direction of the Kaaba in Makkah.
    Tip: You can use a compass or mobile app to find Qibla accurately.
  3. Intention (Niyyah): Make a mental intention in your heart about which prayer you are performing.
    Tip: Niyyah is in the heart and does not need to be spoken aloud.
  4. Takbeer (Raise Hands and Say Allahu Akbar): Raise your hands to your ears and say "Allahu Akbar" to start the prayer.
  5. Qiyam (Standing Position): Recite Surah Al-Fatihah followed by another Surah from the Quran while standing.
    Tip: Read with calmness and understanding.
  6. Ruku (Bowing): Bow down, keeping your back straight, and say "Subhana Rabbiyal Azeem" three times.
  7. Standing After Ruku (Qawmah): Stand up straight and say "Sami Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana lakal hamd."
  8. Sujood (Prostration): Prostrate with your forehead, nose, palms, knees, and toes on the floor. Say "Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" three times.
    Tip: Place your forehead and nose gently on the ground with humility.
  9. Sitting Between Sujood: Sit and say "Rabbighfir li" seeking forgiveness.
  10. Second Sujood: Perform another prostration similar to the first one.
  11. Repeat for Additional Rak’ahs: Depending on the prayer, repeat steps 5–10 for required number of Rak’ahs.
  12. Tashahhud (Sitting for Testimony): In the final Rak’ah, sit and recite the Tashahhud and Salat on the Prophet (peace be upon him).
  13. Salam (Ending the Prayer): Turn your head to the right and left saying "Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah" to complete the Salah.
    Tip: End with focus and gratitude to Allah.

Additional Tips for Kids and Beginners

  • Start with shorter prayers and gradually increase your focus.
  • Practice regularly to build a habit.
  • Learn simple duas to recite during prayer.
  • Smile and greet people politely before and after prayer.
  • Listen to Islamic stories and videos about Salah to understand its importance.

With practice, patience, and sincerity, anyone can perform Salah correctly and gain spiritual benefits. May Allah guide us all to perform our prayers properly.
